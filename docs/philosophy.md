# Philosophy

The operating philosophy of this AI Startup Studio control plane.

## Documentation First

Every evaluation, experiment, and decision is recorded in markdown. If it is not documented, it did not happen. The repository is the single source of truth—not slide decks, not chat threads, not memory.

## Business Before Code

Opportunities are evaluated on market evidence, customer pain, and strategic fit before any implementation begins. Architecture proposals describe intent and trade-offs; they do not contain application code. Building is a decision, not a default.

## Evidence Before Opinions

Claims that influence scoring or decisions require cited evidence: source, date, and metric. Hypotheses are labeled as such. Strong convictions without data are flagged, not rewarded.

## Portfolio Management Over Emotional Attachment

Ideas are assets in a portfolio, not personal projects. Decisions follow thresholds and rules, not sunk-cost reasoning. Killing weak opportunities frees resources for stronger ones.

## Small Experiments

Validation uses the smallest test that can produce a meaningful signal. Prefer interviews, landing pages, and prototypes over full builds. Time-box experiments and define success criteria upfront.

## Fast Validation

Move opportunities through the pipeline quickly. A draft that sits unevaluated for weeks is a process failure. Set review dates and enforce them.

## Fast Termination of Weak Ideas

Kill early when evidence is negative or absent. A high kill rate is healthy—it means the studio is discriminating. Archived opportunities are learning assets, not failures to hide.
